Understand Our Cookies

DishRedo Cookies Policy Details

This Cookies Policy describes how DishRedo ("we," "us," or "our") uses cookies and similar technologies to collect and store information when you visit our website (DishRedo.com). Cookies are small text files that are placed on your computer or mobile device when you visit a website. They are widely used to make websites work, or work more efficiently, as well as to provide reporting information. This policy provides a comprehensive overview of the types of cookies we use, their purpose, and how you can manage your preferences. Your privacy is important to us, and we strive to be transparent about our use of these technologies.

1. What are Cookies?

Cookies are small pieces of data stored on your device (computer or mobile device) when you browse the internet. They are widely used to "remember" you and your preferences, either for a single visit (through a "session cookie") or for multiple repeat visits (using a "persistent cookie"). Cookies are essential for many website functionalities, allowing for personalized experiences, remembering login details, and tracking user behavior for analytics. They play a crucial role in modern web browsing, enhancing convenience and efficiency.

2. How DishRedo Uses Cookies

DishRedo uses cookies for various purposes, including enabling core website functionalities, analyzing site traffic, personalizing your user experience, and supporting our marketing efforts. Our use of cookies is designed to make your interaction with DishRedo more efficient and enjoyable. They help us understand how you navigate our site, which content is most popular, and how we can improve our services to better meet your needs. We leverage these insights to continuously enhance our platform.

3. Types of Cookies We Use: Strictly Necessary Cookies

These cookies are essential for the operation of our website. They enable core functionalities such as security, network management, and accessibility. Without these cookies, certain services you have asked for, like logging into secure areas or using shopping carts, cannot be provided. They are typically set only in response to actions made by you which amount to a request for services, such as setting your privacy preferences, logging in, or filling in forms. You cannot opt out of these cookies as they are vital for the website's basic operation.

4. Types of Cookies We Use: Analytical/Performance Cookies

These cookies collect information about how visitors use our website, for instance, which pages visitors go to most often, and if they get error messages from web pages. These cookies do not collect information that identifies a visitor. All information these cookies collect is aggregated and therefore anonymous. It is only used to improve how a website works. This helps us understand user behavior patterns, identify popular content, and optimize site performance to enhance your browsing experience.

5. Types of Cookies We Use: Functionality Cookies

Functionality cookies allow our website to remember choices you make (such as your username, language, or the region you are in) and provide enhanced, more personal features. For instance, remembering your preferred recipe filters or language settings. These cookies can also be used to remember changes you have made to text size, fonts, and other parts of web pages that you can customize. They enhance user convenience by tailoring the site to your preferences.

6. Types of Cookies We Use: Targeting/Advertising Cookies

These cookies are used to deliver advertisements more relevant to you and your interests. They are also used to limit the number of times you see an advertisement as well as help measure the effectiveness of the advertising campaigns. They are usually placed by advertising networks with the website operator’s permission. They remember that you have visited a website and this information is shared with other organizations such as advertisers. This allows for more personalized ad experiences.

7. First-Party Cookies

First-party cookies are cookies set by the website you are visiting (i.e., by DishRedo.com). These cookies are typically used to enable core functionalities of the site, such as maintaining your login session, remembering your preferences, or collecting anonymous analytics data directly for our use. They are essential for a smooth and personalized experience on our platform, ensuring that your interactions are remembered across visits.

8. Third-Party Cookies

Third-party cookies are cookies set by a domain other than the one you are visiting. This happens when the website you are visiting incorporates elements from other sites, such as images, social media plugins, or advertising content. These cookies are often used for cross-site tracking, advertising, and analytics by external service providers. We carefully select our third-party partners to ensure they adhere to privacy standards.

9. Session Cookies

Session cookies are temporary cookies that remain in the cookie file of your browser only until you leave the site. They are used to allow website operators to link your actions during a browser session. For example, they might remember items you've added to a shopping cart as you navigate different pages. Once you close your browser, these cookies are deleted, ensuring no long-term tracking of your single browsing session.

10. Persistent Cookies

Persistent cookies remain on your device for a set period specified in the cookie's parameters, or until you manually delete them. They are activated each time you visit the website that created that particular cookie. These cookies are used for various purposes, such as remembering your preferences for future visits, maintaining your login state, or tracking your behavior over time for analytics and advertising.

11. How to Control Cookies: Browser Settings

Most web browsers allow you to control cookies through their settings. You can typically configure your browser to notify you when you receive a cookie, giving you the chance to decide whether to accept it. You can also block all cookies or delete existing cookies. Please note that blocking all cookies may impact the functionality of many websites, including DishRedo, potentially limiting your access to certain features.

14. Web Beacons and Pixels

In addition to cookies, we may use other similar technologies like web beacons (also known as "tracking pixels" or "clear GIFs"). These are tiny graphics with a unique identifier, similar in function to cookies, and are used to track the online movements of web users. In contrast to cookies, which are stored on a user's computer hard drive, web beacons are embedded invisibly on web pages and emails. They help us understand user engagement and email effectiveness.

15. Local Storage and Session Storage

These allow websites to store larger amounts of data directly in the browser. Session Storage is temporary and cleared when the browser tab is closed, similar to session cookies. Local Storage is persistent and remains until manually cleared. We may use these for features like saving your progress on a recipe or remembering UI settings.
